About us

ESTABLISHMENT/REGISTRATIONS:

The GARD SL was established in September 2020 and registered with the Ministry of Social Welfare, Gender and Childrenâ€™s Affairs, and the Ministry of Finance and Economic Development as a National None Governmental organization (NGO) in Bo City- Sierra Leone. The organization has a membership of Five (5) founding members with openness to accommodate many willing others desiring to be part of us.

The organization aims to empower marginalized communities in Bo and Kenema Districts, focusing on deprived children and widows. Through enhanced access to affordable education, improved health services, and increased agricultural opportunities, the organization seeks to contribute to overall community development. Governed by a five-member Board of Trustees, the organization's day-to-day operations are managed by key staff, including the Executive Director, Assistant Director, Programs Manager, Admin/Finance Manager, and Admin Assistant, who are accountable to the board.

HISTORY OF THE FOUNDERS/DIRECTORS

My name is Mrs. Alice Bundu, and I was born in Kpanguwama Tongie Gorama Mende, Kenema district. My parents are Alieu Margai and Heijeh Margai. As a young girl, between the ages of 8-10, I attended both the mosque and church with my parents. However, in one dream, I saw a huge hand coming down from the sky and pointing towards the church. This experience led me to attend church only and make a promise to God that I would build a church for my community when I grew up. Despite the difficulties, my husband, Mr. Philip Bundu, and I built a church in my village, which was 95% Muslim.

The construction started in 2000 and ended in 2001. Women from the community supported us by bringing sand for the construction of the church. Upon completion, I decided to open a nursing school for early learning and safety for children. This was because I noticed that parents struggled to care for their children on the farm, especially during the rainy season. The school is a charity that provides free uniforms and meals. In addition to the school, my husband and I decided to help women in four communities with agriculture, namely Kpanguwama, Keima, Mondema, and Tongie. We provided seeds, food for work, and paid for brushing and processing (bags, tapolin). We have been running the school and church for almost three years, and we give God all the glory. We are now embarking on microfinance to empower both men and women who are eligible to help in poverty alleviation. Our organization started as a community-based organization in the year 2000 but transformed into a non-governmental organization in 2001. Our head office is in Bo City.

Short info
